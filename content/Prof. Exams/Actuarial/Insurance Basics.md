## Introduction to Insurance (FAQ)

1. What is Insurance???
Systematic process of risk management where financial loss is expected. The Insurer and insured enter into  a contract to  pay back a loss of object / life that was insured subject to  policy limits and deductibles.


2. What is Premium?? Premium is small sum of money paid by the insured to  the insurer to obtain insurance to his life / assets. (Typically ranges bw: 2-5% of the policy value)


3. How does Insurance work??
Insurance works with a called law of large numbers. As more and more people come into  the coverage, there is more certainty about what the expected claims is likely to be. Loss is not bound be incurred by all the policy holders, however the risk is shared among all via the premium.


4. Risk Management.
- a logical process to manage risk is called risk management.


5. Why Premium is not equal to the policy coverage value?
if this happens, none will buy insurance primarily. Insureds will have the capital enough to  manage the loss and there is no incremental benefit of taking an insurance policy.

Types of Risk Management Techniques.
- Retention: take up any loss that occurs by themselves.
- Insurance: a risk transfer strategy via a sum payment of a premium.
- Mitigate: reduce the loss chance by taking precautions.
- Avoid: terminate and do not enter into that loss.

Law of Large Numbers.
- As the sample size grows, the mean of the estimate will likely get closer to the whole population.

this happens if, there are two  things that happen:
1. Independence.
2. Homogeneity.
